Most ecommerce platforms with an integrated website builder have one defining limitation, no transferability. While choosing a platform, you need to consider the requirements to migrate your website, art products, and customer information to another platform. Recently, ecommerce platforms now allow you to export your product data and even some web page content. Many platforms start with a monthly service charge. There are only a few that offer a free version with ecommerce capabilities included.

There are several ways to establish your online architecture. One option is to create a portfolio website to display your gallery and then have an online store as an independent application. This approach allows you to switch ecommerce platforms without the need to recreate your entire website. The store can be routed to a subdomain like store.mysite.com and accessible from your portfolio website. If you aim to have a dedicated portfolio page, then a low-cost or free ecommerce web page may be ideal. Another option is to build your entire website, including a portfolio, ecommerce store, and even a blog, on a single platform if you find one suitable for your business.

Online infrastructure is probably the last thing on your mind but is an important operational and cost consideration as you expand your business. We will look at two platforms that provide a free version and focus on the website builder, ecommerce features, and payment options of the free tier.

Square Online

Best for Selling Art Online and In-Person

Square Online is a solid choice for artists with small businesses seeking to establish an online presence. Its strengths lie in its simplicity, integration capabilities, and the familiarity it offers to those already using Square for in-person transactions. The drag-and-drop website builder makes it easy to create visually pleasing online stores without coding knowledge, and the platform's mobile responsiveness ensures a smooth experience for customers on various devices.

One of the standout features is the integration with Square's point-of-sale system, allowing artists to seamlessly manage both online and offline sales from a single dashboard. This can be particularly advantageous for artists who participate in events or markets, as it streamlines the entire sales process.

There are some limitations to consider. While Square Online provides customization options, it might not offer the same level of design flexibility as some other platforms. Additionally, while the basic version of the platform is cost-effective, transaction fees can add up over time, impacting profitability for higher-volume sales. The platform's app ecosystem is also less extensive compared to some competitors, which might limit the ability to add certain advanced functionalities.

Pros of using Square Online for Artists

  1. Ease of Use: Square offers a user-friendly interface that makes it accessible for artists to set up and manage their online stores without technical expertise.
  2. Integrated Ecommerce: Artists can seamlessly integrate ecommerce functionality into their websites using Square, allowing them to sell their products directly.
  3. Point of Sale Integration: Square's point of sale system can be beneficial for artists who also sell their work in physical locations, providing a unified selling experience.
  4. Payment Options: Square supports various payment methods, including credit cards and mobile payments, giving customers flexible options.
  5. No Monthly Fees: Square does not charge monthly subscription fees for its basic online store, which can be advantageous for artists on a budget.
  6. Inventory Management: The platform offers tools to manage inventory, helping artists keep track of their artworks and stock levels.
  7. Customer Management: Square provides customer management features, allowing artists to build and maintain relationships with their audience.

Cons of using Square Online for Artists

  1. Limited Customization: While Square offers customization options, artists might find the design and customization capabilities somewhat limited compared to other platforms.
  2. Transaction Fees: Square charges transaction fees for each sale made through their platform, which can add up over time.
  3. Basic Design: The design options might not be as visually impressive or diverse as some other website-building platforms.
  4. Less Extensive Ecommerce Features: While Square provides ecommerce features, artists seeking advanced functionalities might find the options more limited compared to dedicated ecommerce platforms.
  5. Third-Party Integrations: Square's app ecosystem is not as extensive as some other platforms, which could limit the ability to integrate certain third-party tools or services.
  6. Learning Curve for Ecommerce: Artists new to ecommerce might need time to learn and set up the ecommerce features provided by Square.
  7. Less Focus on Artistry: Square is a versatile platform used by various businesses, so artists might need to adapt its features to best suit their creative needs.

Summary

Square Online is a user-friendly and emphasis on simplicity make it a convenient choice for artists and small businesses looking to establish an online store. Its integration with Square's point-of-sale system makes it a simple for physical selling locations. This is a great platform for low-volume sellers, but we would recommend a more scalable platform for artist with high-volume sales.

Big Cartel

Best for Specifically Selling Art Pieces

Big Cartel stands out as a niche platform tailored specifically for artists and makers. Its strengths lie in its simplicity and artist-friendly features. The platform's user interface is intuitive, making it easy for artists to set up and manage their online stores without the need for extensive technical expertise. The focus on customization allows artists to showcase their unique brand and aesthetic, which is crucial in the creative industry.

The affordability of Big Cartel is another advantage, particularly for artists who are just starting their ecommerce ventures. The free plan allows users to list a limited number of products, making it an excellent option for testing the waters or for artists with smaller inventories. The platform's commitment to fostering a community of artists adds a sense of camaraderie, which can be motivating and supportive for creative entrepreneurs.

Big Cartel has its limitations. As an artist-focused platform, it might not offer the same advanced ecommerce features as larger, more comprehensive platforms. Artists looking for complex product variations, extensive analytics, or robust marketing tools might find some of their needs unmet. Additionally, the customization options, while artist-friendly, might be limiting for those seeking highly specific designs.

Pros of using Big Cartel for Artists

  1. Artistic Focus: Big Cartel is specifically designed for artists and makers, making it well-suited for creatives looking to showcase and sell their work.
  2. Simplicity: The platform offers a simple and easy-to-use interface, making it accessible for artists who may not have extensive technical knowledge.
  3. Customization: Big Cartel provides customization options, allowing artists to personalize their online stores and reflect their artistic style.
  4. Affordability: Big Cartel offers pricing plans that cater to artists on a budget, with a free plan available for a limited number of products.
  5. Artist Community: Being artist-focused, Big Cartel provides a sense of community and resources tailored to the needs of creative entrepreneurs.
  6. Mobile Responsiveness: Big Cartel ensures that online stores look and function well on mobile devices, accommodating mobile shoppers.
  7. Integration with Artist Platforms: Big Cartel can integrate with platforms like Instagram, allowing artists to seamlessly promote and sell their work.

Cons of using Big Cartel for Artists

  1. Limited Scalability: While suitable for smaller artists or makers, Big Cartel's features might become limiting as businesses grow or require more advanced functionalities.
  2. Limited Ecommerce Features: The platform provides basic ecommerce features, but artists seeking more advanced capabilities might find them lacking.
  3. Design Limitations: While customizable, artists might find design options somewhat limited compared to other website-building platforms.
  4. Transaction Fees: Some Big Cartel plans charge transaction fees on sales, which could impact profitability, especially for higher-volume sales.
  5. Limited SEO Tools: Big Cartel's SEO features are more basic compared to other platforms, potentially affecting search engine visibility.
  6. App Integration: While Big Cartel offers some app integrations, its app ecosystem might not be as extensive as those of larger ecommerce platforms.
  7. Learning Curve for Ecommerce: Artists new to ecommerce might need time to understand and utilize the ecommerce functionalities provided by Big Cartel.

Summary

Big Cartel is a valuable platform for artists and makers who prioritize simplicity, customization, and community in their online selling journey. Its ease of use and affordability make it a great starting point for emerging artists, but those with more advanced ecommerce needs might need to consider the platform's limitations in terms of features and scalability.
